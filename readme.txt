== patching == (skip this part if you're familiar with how to apply IPS patches and have the whole translation part of things under control)

if this gender-switching romhack for FF3 is somehow the first romhack you've ever used, that's kinda flattering. i hope you like it. the process of applying a romhack in the form of an IPS patch is pretty easy, really. 

you will need a headered ROM of FF3. i will not tell you how to get a headered ROM of FF3. if you are looking at this, you should probably know how to get a headered ROM of FF3. it might have a [!] in its filename.

you can get Lunar IPS, the general standard for IPS patching on modern OSes, from romhacking.net using this link: https://www.romhacking.net/utilities/240/

download that, extract it, run it. it should register IPS as a file type that it runs, so you should be able to just open an IPS file to have it automatically open a browser dialogue - but you can accomplish the same thing by clicking the "Apply IPS Patch" button on LunarIPS and opening your IPS from there. either way, from there you should select your ROM (a headered ROM of FF3). you should get a victorious message after that, indicating that your ROM is patched. now when you play that ROM, your characters should be girls. 

note that this readme is all in the english language. your ROM might not be, yet - an english translation patch for FF3 should be easy for you to find on romhacking.net. there are a lot of them, actually. you can apply them with Lunar IPS as well, the same way as you just did with my patch. it shouldn't matter what order you patch your ROM in, given a translation patch and my gender swap patch affect completely separate sectors of data on it. there is one problem: the translation is for the original FF3, and likely has dialogue that explicitly or implicitly marks your party as boys. this is a complicated issue, that will be revisited in the next section of this readme.


== script revisions == 

i've had people tell me as i worked on this patch that they didn't really even remember ff3 characters being any specific gender, which i Get to an extent. a decent proportion of the job models are at least kinda unisex, some can be generously interpreted as feasibly androgynous, etc. but some are pretty hard to do this with (notably the thief and viking, which feature prominent facial hair), and in any case i wanted to redesign the characters so that you didn't have to do mental acrobatics to interpret your characters as girls. translations, too, tend to translate at least parts of the dialogue with explicitly gendered terminology applied to the party - where and how much depends on the translation, and some can certainly be interpreted as only addressing a few members of the party, but the game certainly seems to assume between a plurality and a totality of the four party members are male. 

to be clear, if one can simply ignore this, the game should be perfectly playable with just the graphics patch applied. if so, great. but probably, most people want there to be a script revision so that they're not getting misgendered all over the place. one big issue here is the plurality of translations that exist for FF3. in the process of playtesting i decided to play through the Alex W. Jackson translation, since it still appears to be the most popular translation (though i kinda like the ChaosRush one more, from what i remember). this allowed me to take note of situations i ran into where the party was called boys, guys, etc, which gave me a chance to make a script hack for it specifically - but other translations still need to be addressed.

in any case, currently you will have to use the Alex W. Jackson translation if you want a gender-swapped script. i have included my (slightly shoddy - more on that in the next section) patch for this purpose in the "Script" folder included - you can apply it the same way you applied the graphics patch. there weren't a ton of changes that had to be made, though figuring out how to edit the game script was a fun task all of its own. if you apply this script, you won't get called "boys" in the two instances i could find where that happens, and most instances of "guys" were changed over as well. 

*** MAKE SURE that you apply this patch AFTER you have already applied the AWJ translation. it won't work properly otherwise. ***

in the future, i hope to make more script revision patches for other translations. unfortunately, the ChaosRush translation appears to have been rescinded from romhacking.net as of the day before i started writing this readme, so i don't know if i'll get to that one. i don't know how ChaosRush (or the Japanese government) feels about that romhack continuing to circulate, under the circumstances. the ad0220 one also seems popular, so i might do it eventually. given that an at least kinda-thorough investigation of what needs to be translated in a game's script given context issues requires essentially a full playthrough, i won't get to that in a bit. in the meantime, if anyone wants to send me sets of screenshots from other translations where they feel a script edit might be appropriate, please feel free and i will credit you accordingly! 

one more note: while the overtly gendered dialog is changed, a lot of things about the resulting script might be peculiar to some. for one thing, it will be heavily implied that the light warriors are lesbians. explicated, really. dancers and princesses will come onto them. also, old guys will challenge their manhood. they will lose. because certain NPCs pull their overworld sprites from the same place as your job classes, falgabard is now populated by an all-female warrior conclave devoted to the dark blade. i do not personally consider this a problem. i will say, if anyone wants to send me screenshots of dialog of interest in different translations, bear in mind that dialog pertaining to these NPCs should probably also reflect female designators, pronouns etc. 

some other parts are a little peculiar too, depending on your perspective. desch will bro around with you the same way he does in the original, including about his own lust for women. so idk, i guess he caught wind that you're gay and wants to bond over your mutual love of women. it has struck me that it could be fun to genderswap desch as well, for this reason. or conversely, to genderswap sarina and have desch be really gay - that might be an extra, later. more likely though, gender swapping certain minor NPCs who show up at the end of the game has occurred to me as potentially important, since they're supposed to be something like mirror images of the party. this is likely to get updated.

== some thoughts on the design philosophy == 

i just wanted to talk a bit about how i approached doing all this. this gets pretty verbose. 

for one thing, this is my first romhack. gender swap romhacks are pretty common these days: there are like four of them for FF1 alone, there's one for contra, there's zynk's roll-chan mod for megaman 1-6 (which i highly recommend), there's one for mother 1, at least 2 for ghouls n ghosts, etc. i felt a genderswap romhack for FF3 had to be on the horizon, but years went by and no such luck.

then i saw PPUX (https://www.romhacking.net/utilities/1871/) and i was like, why don't i just do it? 

i wanted to retain, in essence, the graphical design philosophy that appeared to be at work in the original FF3. a lot of things you see in later games' spritework is informed by newfound graphical capabilities, especially colour depth allowing more detail to be squeezed out of each pixel. i tend to find that attempts to do this on the NES look weird in general, but moreover they kinda look out of place and copy-pasted. i've seen a lot of romhacks where all their female characters look like Lenna or Krile from FF5. i wanted to avoid that, and use an approach that was relatively harmonious with the design philosophy already in place. 

as much as i had "as much like koichi ishii and kazuko shibuya might have thrown into the original game if they were told to make the characters girls as possible" as a design philosophy, i also know my own sensibilities are at work here. the early 90s were the height of popularity for anime fantasy characters with tremendous shoulderpads, an enthusiasm that made its way into FF3 and which i personally don't share. many of my redesigns reduced or disposed of that feature, though some just don't work without it (such as the fighter) so i mostly left it in (ok, i reduced her shoulder pads only a little, but they're honestly still huge). another thing i'll admit: i'm a fashion-conscious millennial woman with pretty feminine overall aesthetic preferences. while i think mostly my guiding philosophy with these redesigns was that they should look like female fantasy characters, it was definitely my interpretation thereof. if someone wants to hit me up with a request for a butch thief or something like that, feel free to make the request. i can definitely put those sorts of things in the Extras folder. 

finally, different characters have been modded to different levels. more unisex or ambiguous designs like the white mage, black mage, geomancer etc just didn't really *demand* much change. that said, i kinda wanted to shift them around a bit anyway (partially cause it felt lazy not to, and partially because i felt the drive), so i did: white mage has some more done-up hair, black mage has FF5-inspired hair and hat baubles, geomancer's clothing has a slightly more feminine cut, just as examples. geomancer is probably the least-modified character, for what that's worth. some other characters got virtually redesigned: the monk and black mage make little nods to their original designs with their similarly distinctive eyes as well as the black mage's cape and scar-like blue mark, but other than that they have nearly redesigned-from-scratch clothing and hair. the original viking design is like 50% beard, so reworking her was a lot of fun, honestly.

finally, i have some little thoughts on what inspired each character below! (which is another opportunity to stop reading, if you're not interested in that side of things)

-

Onion Kid: The original kinda just looks like A Kid, but i wanted to make it more emphatically A Girl, so i gave her a pinafore-ish dress, a little dangle of hair underneath her helmet, and a bit of a flair to accentuate her helmet because i felt it suited her. nothing elaborate, but i think the result is cute!

Fighter/Warrior: the first redesign i did, she got redesigned a few times as i worked on the game. in the end, i wanted her to retain the kinda bulky, muscular build of the original fighter while still being more clearly A Girl. i kept her hair the same sort of iconic mess as the original fighter, but did it up a little more stylish with a forward sweep and headband. she has a more complete, tunic-like skirt and less exaggeratedly wide boots (another aesthetic tendency of the time i'm not very into), but maintains a lot of the top-heavy appeal of the original design.

Monk: being my second redesign, i remember jumping through hoops to figure out how this character should work - the amount of reconfiguring my approach was, i think, a contributing factor in how dissimilar she is from the original monk. my first draft of her wound up having weirdly scrawny legs and some of her clothing moved weirdly in her animations, perhaps an indication that i'd departed too much from what the original designers had found to work. after several revisions i eventually ended up with one of the redesigns i'm proudest of. i think the light scarf, the two-piece gi and messy buns make her a bit cute, but also capable of crushing ribcages. i'm still not sure if the scarf might be causing her head to look a little flat, though.

White Mage: not much to say here. a lot of people already headcanon this design as female, even though i don't think it was the designers' intention. that said, i gave her some tasseled braids, inspired a bit by the FFT female WM design, which i think are cute and characteristic.

Black Mage: similarly to the white mage, the black mage kinda comes off as genderless. regardless, i took some cues from FF5 in giving her some hair dangling from the shadows, and while i couldn't use the moon motif from FF5 cause Magus already does that, i did give her a little hat bauble.

Red Mage: i've always had an affection for the FF1 red mage's design and mechanics, even if they were a little silly and ineffectual. i tend to identify with "combat mage" archetypes sometimes, i think for this reason. in any case i gave her a lot of my sensibility here, with a cute skirt and a somewhat more vogue approach to her hat, which now has a band holding the feather in place. consulting FF5 red mage lenna's design helped me figure out some of the proportions. 

Ranger/Hunter: look at this little dweeb lmao. honestly the original model for this character looks pretty unisex *aside* from the victory animation. similar to the fighter i gave her a more complete skirt, and i reeled in the weirdly baggy sleeves around the shoulders a bit. i gave her a little braid as well. i love her little victory pose, now - testing it in PPUX always put a smile on my face, and i often would leave her in that animation in a corner of the PPUX interface while i worked on the other sprites. i hope you love her too.

Knight: god she looks so cool imo. i lowered her circlet a bit to frame her face and hair better, and gave her hair some glitzy ornaments, and a very swanky outfit. i don't really know what to say, even. she looks kinda like a model, but like, the kinda model u can talk to and she actually holds good conversation. that's what i think anyway.

Thief: you know, it's funny because i feel like this was one of the most straightforward adaptations even though she looks completely different. as usual, i toned down the ridiculous shoulderpads, gave her a fuller skirt, adjusted her hair into something more stylish (yet still appropriately edgy), and widened her bandanna a bit so it'd be appropriate for her fuller hair length. it was all pretty straightforward! does she have big sister energy? i feel like she does.

Scholar: she looks like a hot teacher, which is about what i was going for. again, i actually didn't change that much about her - i gave her more of a shaggy bang, gave her a pencil skirt in place of the pants, made her robe flare a little more... i mean, the overworld sprites almost look the same as the old ones. but it does the trick, right? it just left me wishing i could give her glasses, but alas, NES graphical limitations don't take kindly to that.

Geomancer: as mentioned earlier, this is basically just a shadowy little gremlin person. it seems pretty genderless by default. her outfit is a bit more shaped and dress-like, with maybe a little less bulky fabric impressions. i have thought about playing with her hair a bit, but i think it looks fine as it is.

Dragoon: this one was a little tough. dragoons are quite iconically a humanoid lump of armor, and FF3 was the first to set that down. in the end, though, i actually found a fair bit to play with, giving her a FF5-inspired (again) section of hair at the front of her helmet, changing the helmet's position a bit, going for a rectangular front skirt rather than a triangular one (funny how that keeps happening, since it's a similar approach to how i changed the fighter, ranger and thief) and reshaped/redecorated her armor a bit. in the end i think she looks pretty natural.

Viking: i love the way this goober turned out. since the original design was defined by a gigantic beard, i had to figure out what would fill all that space, and i went with a big, wide belt drawn over her shoulder. more iconically, i reshaped her helmet a bit to give it holes underneath the horns, through which her braided pigtails are drawn. the overall "vibe" i was getting from the design as i worked on her made it feel all too appropriate: she now throws finger guns on victory. also, is it just me or does she look a little like an akira toriyama design? something about the goofy helmet, i dunno.

Black Belt/Karateka: much like the monk, she's one of my faves. given that the original black belt and monk obviously are meant to bear some resemblance, i used the same principle here. she has the same distinctive eyes and martial arts-inspired outfit, though now it's more a kind of fantasy-ified cheongsam and her hair's pulled together in neater buns. she borrows the cape, as well as the scar-like blue mark on her face from the original black belt design. i changed the build up/victory animation as well, to an extended palm with an aura of energy - i just always found the ambiguous lump coming from the original design's fist pretty uninspired. i don't think it's Too Much.  she just looks so cool, ugh. imo.

Dark Knight/Mystic Knight: again, kind of a weird one. i suppose there's nothing especially gendered in the fundamental sense about the original design, but it still has a masculine-leaning vibe. i looked at other female dark knight designs from later FF games, and all of them were either too elaborate to look good (FF10-2 for instance) or not really feasible to adapt to the limitations of the NES (like the FFT WotL dark knight's skirted design). when i looked for other artist depictions of such a character, i found a lot of designs with long, open-front skirts, which i figured i could adapt to my own uses - and this was the result. i like her well enough, though i still feel like more could be done to really make her stand out. that said, i'm not unhappy with her. and, i do think one funny side-effect of this hack is that falgabard is now an enclave of all-female disciples of the dark blade. 

Evoker/Conjurer: i got the idea of turning the single, stiff sprout from the original design into two longer, floppier ones pretty immediately, but my first attempt kinda ended up looking like 90s hair instead. like, the kind of 90s hair that gives the impression that she considers herself a raver even though she's gone to two raves and both times were with her mom. the kind of 90s hair that says "the beats are pumpin and the people are bumpin". anyway, i fixed it. she has leafy little antennae now. fwiw, i think people sleep on this job. you can shut down a lot of battles in a single "support" cast of shiva or ramuh, for instance. i know this isn't a guide, i'm just saying.

Bard: probably a more extensive edit than one would expect. i made her hair a little more full-bodied, less like the mop possessed of the original design. the ambiguously plant-like ribbon has been replaced with a long, flowy hairband. she's got a loose dress a bit inspired by FF5 lenna's bard design, and i changed her dance-like cheer/singing animation into something a bit less fist-pumpy. it took a while to get that last part to look right but with a whole lot of editing, i nailed it eventually and i really like her cheer, now - almost as much as the ranger. i hope there are people out there who actually play FF3 bards enough for it to matter! (cheer is good sometimes...)

Magus/Warlock: much like the black mage she's based on, there wasn't a ton to change here, at least conceptually. i gave her some hair, similar to the black mage, though the different hat position kinda messed with that. in experimenting with things, i eventually noticed i liked the idea of giving her a slightly jauntier kind of hat, with a thick brim and "star"-like ornamentation that blinks in tandem with the "moon" from the original design during the casting/victory animation. this was probably the most "i hope people don't dislike this fanciful change i made" decision i made throughout the process, i kinda just felt changing nothing but giving her a little hair was unsatisfying. idk. maybe i'll make a version without this change later.

Devout/Shaman: another fairly unisex original design, i decided to make the oversized tudor shirt-ish outfit of the original design a bit more dress-like and took some notes from devout refia's design in the DS remake, such as longer boots and adapting the cape into a sleeved mantle, in the process. i think the result is cute! also, in doing the overworld sprites, i gave her a little cat pounce for her left and right walking animations. 

Summoner: now here's one i feel strongly about. maybe it was the novelty of giving the character a horn or something - after all, that's since stuck as a staple part of the FF summoner design - but isn't the rest of the original design really uninspired? it looks like he's wearing a green potato sack with a down arrow on it. so in my redesign, i did the obligatory stuff like giving her some fancier, less boyish hair, but i also made her outfit into something more like what we expect of a summoner design in an FF game since FF5. i did incorporate a bit of a reference to the down arrow-looking symbol on the original design, at least, but overall i kinda completely revamped this character's outfit. and i like the results, too!

Sage: this one was really weird. i stated that part of my design goal was to do something similar to what the character and graphics designers would have done if the light warriors in FF3 were intended to be girls, but with the sage, the pixel art doesn't really follow the game's own standards. while FF3 characters (and indeed, FF characters in general through to FF5) are typically drawn in profile in combat with one leg behind the other so that they appear to just merge into one, the sage very specifically has one leg out in front while the other is implied to be under their cape, with their toe just peeking out. this made redesigning the character kind of weird. i even thought about redesigning the character mostly from scratch with the normal stance, but figured that was excessive - it was just hard to get the right type of detail i wanted to make my changes. in the end, it turned out giving her another pixel of height helped a fair bit. i think the main reason they made this character so short in the first place was to accommodate the gigantic hat, which would overflow the sprite's borders during the hurt animation otherwise. i didn't really think this mattered - i just drew my version of the hurt animation so that the hat was at a lower position, falling over her face a little more. in any case, in any case, she now has the obligatory FF boob pixels, slightly more decorative hair, and her cape is more like a cloak now. while the changes were actually pretty extensive, i feel like the end results are actually pretty subtle in their differences from the original design. which i think is good, all things considered! again, this was one of the more unisex models originally.

Ninja: as mentioned in the "extras" section, this character got re-redesigned part way through the playtesting experience, when i noticed i could take notes from the kunoichi enemy's design. it wasn't a huge re-redesign, at least. i'd already gone with the idea of giving her the whole "ninja gi worn as a dress" thing that's pretty common with fantasy ninja girls. my original model wears a pair of geta, which i thought was a nice touch, and hardly a thing you don't see with media depiction of ninjas, though tabi are more common. it also used a similar kind of mesh to what's used in the original ninja's sleeve design as part of the skirt - which in retrospect i'm not sure worked either. in any case, i kinda preferred the kunoichi-inspired redesign, which is why it's the one in the base patch while my original design is in the extras. that said, i still like my original (i think both of them look really cool!), so i figured it was worth including. it can perhaps be noted that i (painstakingly, since afaik there's no way to move graphics within and across tiles in PPUX) did add an extra pixel of height to this character so that the geta would fit, and in making the adaptation to the kunoichi-based design, i just left that in. i think it looks fine, and it makes sense since women are biologically taller than men on average.
